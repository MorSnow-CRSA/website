Monitoring and Quantifying Snowmelt contribution for Moroccan Water Management strategies in the context of Climate Change

MorSnow Project

About MorSnow

The principal rivers in Morocco originate from the mountain ranges (Atlas and Rif). These regions play the role of a water tower for the downstream lands by providing a significant volume of the superficial water for aquifers alimentation and dam recharge. The agriculture sector is considered as the primary consumer of the supplied water. Due to the orographic effect, air temperature decreases significantly by altitude, and precipitation falls as liquid or solid forms. Consequently, head watersheds with elevations above 1400 meters are covered with snow and receive an enhanced precipitation rate in snowfall in the cold season. As measured on the southern (Mgoun) and the northern (Ourika) slopes of the High Atlas range, snowfall represents between 20% and 80% of total precipitation depending on the elevation and season.

Considering water scarcity and climate change constraints, proper water resources management is becoming a national priority. It is necessary to understand and quantify water fluxes redistribution at the catchment scale to design and implement accurate management plans for water resources. Despite the importance of the snow component, high watersheds are almost devoid of ground observations causing a gap in water budget calculations. Remote sensing technologies proved to provide reliable information to monitor snow cover areas (SCA).

MorSnow's vision

The MorSnow Project will directly compensate for the gap in ground data. MorSnow will develop decision support tools for improving water resources management, from experimental to larger basins, by integrating satellites images into hydrological models to quantify and forecast water balance components in the upstream parts of reservoirs and basins in Morocco. The research in MorSnow focuses on

  • Measuring the meteorological and snowpack parameters and analyzing their spatial and temporal dynamics by using both automatic records and field surveys.
  • Measuring snow sublimation through the installation of an Eddy covariance device on snow Satellite-based monitoring of snow to assess the effect of different physical and climatic factors on snow cover area distribution
  • Modeling snow accumulation
  • Using UAV-TIR Camera to constrain snowmelt models at the local and small basin scale
  • Isotopic sampling to assess the recharge processes from snow and rain
  • Test the usefulness of the GRACE satellite to monitor groundwater recharge
  • Runoff and hydrological modeling to estimate the water balance within the basin
  • Assessing the impact of the possible future changes in climatic conditions on water resources (both in terms of surface water and groundwater)

Description of Oukaimden Plateau
Description of Oukaimden AWS
A team of researchers conducted a data collection campaign in Oukaimden, focusing on measuring snow height, density, and related variables. They gathered measurements of snow height, density, and related variables. Using specialized tools, the team collected data at multiple locations to assess the characteristics of the snowpack.
The experimental works aimed to contribute to ongoing studies and inform snow-related activities in the region.
